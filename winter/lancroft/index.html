Our main character is the caregiver for her dying husband. She sits at his side in the hospital as his mind travels through the muses and demons of his life.

He was aware of her by his side as he drifted in and out of consciousness.

Adorned in feathers attached to a nylon parachute outfit, the crew is filming a scene for a television show which reproduces a Mayan blood letting ritual - a practice that allowed the priests and priestesses to communicate with their ancestors by inhaling blood from Ayahuasca soaked ropes in the high altitudes of the Andes.

Before the husband took ill, the couple had made their wealth producing realty TV shows which focused on bringing together brilliant minds from science, business and exploration. They placed them in beautiful environments with drinks and abundant muses to tempt and inspire while the world tuned in weekly to watch the groups talk, argue and come up with inspired ideas to solve world problems.

The couple was constantly busy juggling work, travel and the needs of their children. Ultimately it was their own lives that were most in need of solutions.

She was not always happy, but the stories they told made the world feel young again, like children, so it was well worth the ride. And with a whoshhh he was airborne, flying high over the Caribbean blue below, forever lost, as most husbands seem to be.

2011-07-23 Carried me to the bridge

Willem has a dream while leading a Travel School course in the Yucatan…

Maya Bridge

I awoke with the sudden rush of awareness.  The flowing bands of DNA-like strands were not you and me, it was you and [Teresa].  It was the strength of two women and all women.  It made perfect sense, the braiding, spinning double helix was femininity, and I was the agent of creation and destruction.  For the first time, my foot alighted firmly on the bridge guided by your form.  From out of the water the towering waves of life sprung infinitely upward and outward.  The bridge was both contemporary and ancient.  Your combined strength placed me there. You were the cat, agile and twisting. And I was the bird, chattering and aligned only by your grace.  How foolish of me to think that I was holding you in my flight, that we alone were the only ones in flight. 

Foot on the bridge, hand on your form, now I saw that the flowing hair emanated from not just two beings, but from many more. It was the path of life, connecting everything born as one solid flow – combining in the trunk of the present – held in the now and spreading outward in all directions.  Strands intertwined, and connected to a zillion more rising from the water below – the past from which your roots sprung.  Your hair flowed outward to futures we had not yet traversed, with patterns were instantly knowable across all time.  In one heart-felt leap, I spun with my arm around your combined trunk, outward and over the river of life below.  I was one with the true tree of life and all its power.  Here I, the outsider, here I the male, held tight to all inception, to all birth and to all death simultaneously.  And in that moment the evolution to winged man had begun.  To man united with grace.

Austen [or Kali] came to me yesterday as we were preparing to leave and said, “Dad what if there was a superhero who used his hair as his super power.  What if it was like Spiderman’s web, and it could grow for swinging from building to building?”  I said, “Would he need to cut it off each time, or would it just continue to grow?”  We were crouched on the floor surrounded by hundreds of Abbeys Barbie dolls, some very old 70’s dolls handed down from Terri and her sister.  Cher was in my hands, and Wonder Woman was in Abbey’s hands.  Abbey had just asked me if we should trim Wonder Woman’s crazy kinky flowing hair, and I’d said, “No, let it grow, it’s where she gets her power from.”

When you confirmed to me that you were my muse now, like you’d been for many others when I first knew you, it felt like a slight because I knew you were much more.  I had to make the leap to catch you before our connection escaped me.  I clung to you and now you’ve carried me to the bridge, standing there firmly only briefly to gain perspective, before leaping back into your spinning eternity.  Consciousness held flow in its arms.  I could see that you were one, and I could see the strength of your connection to earth and sky.  This is something new for you because you have not always had a strong connection to other women.  You’ve been disconnected and a drift like I am from men.  I’m reaching to embrace you and reconnect you with the whole.

From here the next step for me is to head down the tree to its roots, to explore where it meets the water’s edge.  I have to learn about the destructive forces that tore asunder the relationship between my father and my mother.  I hope to find more clues in Kitty Hawk.  Wait a second, Kitty Hawk, Bird Jaguar.  Bird Jaguar, Kitty Hawk. Both a bird and a cat.  Weird.

Why is Kitty Hawk significant to me?  When I was four years old my mother, father and I came here from Illinois to camp by the beach, pulling our small blue tent-camper behind the Pontiac.  We nestled our camper in the sandy campsite among the trees.  My mom caught a crab off the pier, using a chicken head as bait.  It chattered and changed a rainbow of hues as she boiled it without heating the water beforehand.  Rat-ta-tat-tat-tat, it’s tiny claws clattered for life in the aluminum pan over the camp stove.  That night, 70 mile-an-hour winds buffeted our tent as a hurricane blew near, only miles from shore.  My dad moved slowly through the storm, his 6’1” form hunched against the wind for stability, rope in hand lashing our tent camper to the surround trees, and I said “Big Wind, Big Wind, Big Wind” over and over again.

What I remember most fondly about Kitty Hawk is the tall hill that towers over the area where Wilbur and Orville took flight.  Shaped like a Mayan pyramid, it rises high above the beach and water below.  A hill of steep grass, perfect for a child to climb and tumble down.  It’s hard not to share this with Patrick now, but we will have many hills to climb and tumble on in the years to come.  And you will tell him the story of Bird Jaguar so he knows the legend woven from the fibers of the love around him.

When you come in the fall, we will plant corn in the tradition of Demeter the daughter of Persephone.  We will dance in the moon light.

Austen said he dreamed about two pirates kids on a beach trying to steal a treasure chest buried in the sand from two guards. He didn't know who the other boy was with him - maybe your son. One of the boys distracted the guards while the other boy dragged the treasure to their ship which was anchored just off the shore. I told him there would be a pirate museum in Kitty Hawk, he didn't know before the dream.

Terri said to me yesterday that Austen and I should be in a band together, father and son.  Perhaps the winged bird men with hair of gold; chattering, chattering at your window bold.

A man on the street, says “come to me” to a woman, “I will make things better.”  He is a fool because he is selfish, he seeks disruption rather than balance.  But the surrounding community forces his hand.  He cannot unite families under the rules of our society.  He can only remove a father from a son. That’s the disruption that led my mother to leave my dad, and her mother to leave her prior husband.  My mother – torn loose by a fellow jogger who managed a factory that made car windows.  My grandmother – glimpsed by my grandfather Eugene, a car salesman who saw her walk past the glass showroom window of a Packard dealership in Detroit.  Simply put, a relation of two will always create a polarity, a discontent.   The problem is when we overlook the forming minds of the children within the relationships.  The ripping energy is immortal, but with the right story telling mechanism the fabric for balance is introduced.  Matriarchal groups have maintained this balance and stability for eons, to protect and raise children.

I have decided that I will introduce you as my Adopted Sister. Have a wonderful Saturday my sweet muse of forgotten memories. ;)

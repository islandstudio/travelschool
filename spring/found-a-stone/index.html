From pre-history, we move forward to 1603.  A young European man, Piet Hein, is peering through the jungle, watching a Conquistador talk to a native man.  The native man says, “Why are you here conquistador? You take our gold, you take our women, what more do you want?”

“I want the stone.”

“What stone?” the native man replies.

“The Memory Stone” says the conquistador.

“It’s not here. It’s already gone.” Lifting his gaze and looking into the distance, several ships are seen disappearing over the horizon.

The Dutch man watching from the jungle is a young Piet Hein.  The Spanish man hears him move and soon Piet is caught.  As a captive he is put to work as a galley-slave, where he learns Spanish.  Later he is swapped in a prisoner trade and returns to the Netherlands where he tells a group of wealthy Dutch investors the stories of the memory stone.  With their funding he assembles a Dutch armada to capture the Spanish Silver fleet off the coast of Cuba.

But finding the stone alludes him.  Without finding it, Captain Hein lets the Spanish sailors go.  The Spanish walk to Havana as he returns to the Netherlands with the gold.  The stone had been held in the beak of a bird high above in the trees while the men were searched.

Back to the present day, several families are walking on the beach.  Two young boys find the stone in the sand.  One boy’s father, Willem, immediately recognizes it.  The older boy’s mother, Tess, asks what they’ve found. 

On closer inspection, the stone the boys found does not look like the one Willem thought he recognized from his reading of Piet's journal.
